First, you’ll want to make sure your screen is showing exactly what you want to capture. Once you’ve got the display just right, press the ‘Windows key’ + ‘PrtScn’ on your keyboard to take a screenshot. Your laptop will now save the picture to the Pictures folder in your computer.

Third, if you only want to capture a selected area of your screen, press ‘Windows key’ + ‘Shift’ + ‘S’ and the screen will turn into a grey overlay. Click and drag with your mouse to select the part you want to grab. Once done, press ‘Ctrl’ + ‘V’ to paste the screenshot into your image editor.

Fourth, if you need to take a screenshot of the active window on your computer, press ‘Alt’ + ‘PrtScn’. The screenshot will be copied to your clipboard, from there you can paste it into any document or image editor of your choice.

Finally, you can save yourself some time by pressing ‘Windows key’ + ‘PrtScn’ buttons together. This will automatically save a screenshot of your full screen to the Pictures folder on your laptop.
