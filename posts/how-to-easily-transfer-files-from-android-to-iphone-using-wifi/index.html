Transferring files from Android to iPhone can be done quickly and easily with WiFi, the ‘Send Anywhere’ app, or Dropbox. Follow the steps outlined in this article and you’ll be able to transfer files in no time.

Connecting Android and iPhone Devices to the Same Network#

Transferring files from your Android device to your iPhone can be done via wifi. It’s a simple and efficient way to keep your data up-to-date on both devices. In this article, we’ll walk you through the process of transferring files from an Android device to an iPhone using wifi.

To begin, you’ll need to make sure both devices are connected to the same wifi network. Once you’ve established that connection, open the ‘Settings’ menu on the Android device and go to ‘Connections’. Select the ‘WiFi’ option and enter the details for the wifi network you want to connect to.

Once you’ve connected your Android to the same wifi as your iPhone, open the ‘Files’ app on your Android device. Navigate to the file you want to transfer. Tap and hold the file until a menu appears, then select ‘share’ from the menu. You’ll be presented with a list of options, select ‘Bluetooth’ and then look for the iPhone on the list of available devices.

Your iPhone should now be listed as one of the available devices. Tap on it and the file transfer will begin. Wait until it is complete and the file will appear in the ‘Files’ app on your iPhone.

This is how you transfer files from an Android device to an iPhone using wifi. It’s a simple process that can save you time and ensure your data is kept in sync across both devices.
