1. Understand the Screenshot Shortcut#

Taking a screenshot on iPhone 11 SE is incredibly easy and just requires a few steps. First, understand the screenshot shortcut. Next, you can edit the screenshot and add annotations to the image. Tap the screenshot thumbnail at the bottom left corner of the screen and the image will open up in the Markup Viewer. You can use the available tools to draw, highlight, add text and even crop and resize your image. When finished, simply tap Done at the top right corner and it will be saved to your camera roll.

If you want to share your screenshot with your friends and family, simply tap the thumbnail at the bottom left corner of the screen. From there, you can select the sharing option of your choice, including AirDrop, Messages, Mail, and Notes.

Furthermore, you can record a video of your screen. To do this, press and hold the power button and volume up button until you see the prompt to start recording. The recording will be saved in the Photos app and you can replay, trim, and share your screen recording like any other video.

Finally, you can capture the entire screen view quickly with the AssistiveTouch option. To enable the feature, go to Settings > Accessibility > Touch > AssistiveTouch and toggle it On. AssistiveTouch will appear as a small circle at the bottom corner of the screen which you can tap to bring up the options. From here you can select the Screenshot option to snap the entire screen view.
