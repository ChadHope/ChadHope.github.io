1. Preparing to Mirror#

Screen mirroring is an easy way to share content between devices, and Panasonic Android TV makes it easier. To setup screen mirroring on your Panasonic Smart TV, first make sure that your device is connected to the same wifi network as the TV. Then, on your TV, under Home > Settings, choose Network & Internet > Miracast. Once Miracast is selected, choose “Enable Wireless Display” and select your device from the list. After that, your device should be automatically connected to your TV and you can start mirroring your device’s screen. To disconnect, just go to the same screen and select your device and it should disconnect.

If your phone is not compatible with Miracast, you can use the Panasonic TV Remote 2 app on your device to control your TV. This app will allow you to mirror content from your phone or tablet to the TV. To use the app, simply download it from the App Store and then connect it to your TV by entering the code shown on the TV. After that, you will be able to mirror content from your device to the TV, as well as control the TV using your device.

For more advanced users, it is also possible to access the apps on your Panasonic Smart TV from your laptop or PC. To do this, you will need to enable the DLNA/UPnP service on your TV. To do this, go to Home > Settings > Network & Internet > Advanced Options and select “Enable DLNA/UPnP”. After that, you can access the apps on your Panasonic Smart TV from your PC or laptop.

In conclusion, setting up screen mirroring on your Panasonic Smart TV is easy and straightforward. With just a few steps, you can start mirroring your device’s screen and accessing your TV’s apps from your laptop or PC.

2. Connecting to the TV#

Screen mirroring is a convenient way to connect your smartphone to your Panasonic Android TV. With this feature, you can easily display what is on your phone onto the larger screen of your television. Here are the steps to follow to set up screen mirroring in your Panasonic Android TV.

First, enable screen mirroring on your Panasonic Android TV. Access the Home menu of the device by pressing the Home button on the remote. Select Setting and navigate to the Network option. Choose Screen Mirroring and select On.

Second, connect your smartphone to your TV. Turn on your smartphone and select Settings. Choose Wireless & networks and activate Wi-Fi direct. Next, go to the Home menu of the TV and select Screen Mirroring. Then, select Connect Now to search for your smartphone. Once found, enter the same PIN on both devices to establish a connection.

Third, access content from your smartphone. Go to the Home menu of the TV and select Screen Mirroring. Select the device name of your smartphone to cast the phone’s display onto the TV’s screen. Then, access the content on your phone that you want to be mirrored on the TV’s screen.

Finally, disconnect the devices. To reset your television, go to the Home menu and select Setting. Choose Network and select Screen Mirroring. Then, turn off the feature. To disconnect your smartphone, disconnect the Wi-Fi direct connection from the phone’s Settings. That is all you have to do to set up screen mirroring in your Panasonic Android TV.
