I. 5 Steps to Easily Edit Screenshots on Steam#

Editing screenshots on Steam is not as complicated as it seems. In this tutorial, we’ll walk through five simple steps to quickly take screenshots and then edit them in Steam’s built-in editing tool. First, we’ll use Steam’s shortcut keys to capture the screenshot. Second, we’ll open the screenshot with the built-in editing tool. Third, we’ll add annotations, cropping, and filters. Fourth, we’ll save the screenshot to various locations. Finally, we’ll post the screenshot directly to the Steam Community. Follow the tutorial and you’ll have your edited screenshots in no time.

To capture screenshots, press the F12 key or right-click the Steam icon in the taskbar and select Take Screenshot. The screenshot will save to your screenshot folder. To open the screenshot in the built-in Steam editing tool, open your Steam library, select the screenshot, and click the Edit button.

Once your screenshot is opened in the editing tool, you can add annotations, cropping, and filters. To add annotations, use the Text and Draw tools. To crop the screenshot, click the Crop tool and drag the edges to select the area you want to keep. Lastly, apply a filter with the Filters tool.

Once you are finished editing the screenshot, save it to various locations. To save it to your computer, click the Export button. To save it to your Steam library, click the Save button. To post the screenshot directly to the Steam Community, click the Share button.

With these five steps, you can easily edit screenshots on Steam! Whether you are taking screenshots for game reviews or creating snapshots of your gaming highlights, you can easily apply annotations, cropping, and filters to make your screenshots look great.
